Finer granularity perturbations. Our sketch considers as perturbations the eight corners of the RGB color cube. While it is very eﬀective in practice, it sometimes misses successful adversarial examples obtained by other RGB values. We next explain how to extend OPPSLA to support ﬁner granularity perturbations. Given a level  of granularity, for  ∈{0, 1, 2,... }, OPPSLA considers for every color channel the values 0, 1 2  , 2 2  ..., 2  2  . For example, for  = 0, it considers the values 0, 1, for  = 1, it considers 0, 1 2 , 1, and for  = 2, it considers 0, 1 4 , 1 2 , 3 4 , 1. That is, for granularity , the number of RGB values is ( 2  + 1) 3 , i.e., every increase of  increases the number of perturbations exponentially. While one can extend the possible perturbations in the priority queue , this would result in a very high number of queries to the network, thus defeating the goal of our sketch. Instead, OPPSLA trades oﬀ success rate and number of queries and limits the number of pixel locations for which it considers ﬁner granularity perturbations to MAX_G (a hyper-parameter).
